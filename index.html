Radioactive Waste for Different Reactors

A demo of radioactive.js

This plot compares radiation levels of waste from three different reactors over the next 10 billion years.

— The black line shows the radiation levels coming from typical waste from a standard Pressurized Water Reactor. The starting mixture of waste isotopes was taken from measurements at this German reactor.

— The green line shows the radiation levels coming from hypothetical reactor with "three times less waste" than a standard Pressurized Water Reactor. It's shown here to illustrate how useless the statement "three times less waste" is. In this case the starting mixture of waste isotopes was taken from measurements at the same German reactor, but the amount of uranium-238 waste (more than 90% of the waste by weight) was reduced by a factor of three.

— The orange line shows the radiation levels coming from a hypothetical Actinide Burner Reactor. The starting mixture of waste isotopes was taken from measurements at the same German reactor, but with all actinides heavier than uranium removed, because theoretically they would be used up in the reactor.
